# Case Study: RCKT — Kresladi PDUFA (B11)

## Event
- **Date:** June 17, 2022
- **Catalyst:** Kresladi (giroctocogene fitelparvovec) PDUFA — gene therapy for hemophilia A
- **Batch:** B11 Adversarial T+7 Challenge
- **Playbook:** E (SC=6, secondary event thesis)

## Setup
Kresladi was FDA-approved. Stock sold off post-approval on overhang clearing and profit-taking from investors who had held through the lengthy approval process. The setup had SC=6 — below the standard Playbook A/B threshold — but qualified under the SC6 Playbook E exception because:
- Tier 1B (sell-the-news approval)
- SC9 clean (no secondary event)
- Institutional ownership intact
- Cash runway >24 months

## Result
- T+7: **+18.0%** — largest SC=6 return in the entire B1–B11 backtest

## Key Lesson
SC=6 is not a reflexive block. The SC6 exception rule exists precisely for setups like RCKT: clean Tier 1A/1B event, no secondary contamination, institutional sponsorship intact. The exception is validated by evidence.

## v2.0 Rule Reinforced
SC6 exception rule (v2.0 Upgrade #9): SC=6 with Tier 1A/1B event + clean SC9 is structurally sound under Playbook E.
